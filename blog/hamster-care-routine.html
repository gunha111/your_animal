🐹 Hamster Care

The Complete Hamster Care Routine
Daily, Weekly & Monthly

⏱ 8 min read · First-Time Owner Guide

Hamsters look simple. And in many ways they are — but "simple" doesn't mean "no care needed." A well-cared-for hamster lives a full 2–3 years, stays healthy, and becomes genuinely friendly. A neglected one gets sick fast, bites out of stress, and often doesn't make it to year two.

This routine covers everything you actually need to do — broken into daily, weekly, and monthly tasks so nothing gets missed.

⚠️ The #1 hamster mistake

Getting a cage that's too small. Hamsters run 5–8 miles per night in the wild. The minimum cage size is 40cm × 70cm floor space — not the tiny cages sold at most pet stores. A larger cage means a calmer, healthier hamster.

🌅 Daily Care Routine

🌙 Evening (6–8 PM) — When hamsters wake up

6:00 PM
Fresh water Refill the water bottle or bowl with clean, room-temperature water. Check that the bottle spout isn't clogged.
6:15 PM
Evening meal About 1–2 teaspoons of hamster mix. Remove any leftover fresh food from earlier (it spoils fast). Check their cheek pouches aren't impacted.
7:00 PM
Handling time (10–15 min) Let them wake up fully before handling. Sit on the floor so they can't fall far. Build trust gradually — don't grab from above.
8:00 PM
Small treat (optional) A small piece of fresh vegetable: broccoli, carrot, cucumber, or bell pepper. Keep portions tiny — hamsters are prone to diabetes.

☀️ Morning (Quick check)

Morning
Spot clean the cage Remove any uneaten fresh food, soiled bedding near the toilet corner. Hamsters pick one corner to use — clean that area daily.
Morning
Visual health check Observe while they sleep. Wheezing, discharge from eyes/nose, wet tail, or lethargy = vet visit immediately.

📅 Weekly Care Routine

Weekly Tasks

Partial bedding change — Replace about 1/3 of the bedding, keeping some that smells familiar so your hamster doesn't get stressed. Don't clean everything at once.

Clean food bowl and water bottle — Scrub with hot water and a drop of dish soap. Rinse thoroughly. Bacteria build up fast in moist environments.

Wipe down cage walls — Use a pet-safe disinfectant or diluted white vinegar. Dry completely before putting your hamster back.

Check wheel for squeaks or damage — A squeaky wheel disturbs your sleep and theirs. Add a drop of olive oil to the axle if needed.

Rotate toys and accessories — Hamsters get bored with the same layout. Rearrange tunnels, add new cardboard, or change hiding spots.

Weigh your hamster — A gram scale check weekly helps catch illness early. Sudden weight loss is often the first sign of a health issue.

📆 Monthly Care Routine

Monthly Tasks

Full cage deep clean — Remove everything, wash the cage with hot water and soap, dry fully. Replace all bedding. Do this once a month (not more — it stresses them out).

Check nails — If they're curling, it's time for a trim. Use small animal nail clippers. If unsure, ask a vet or groomer.

Check teeth — Hamster teeth grow continuously. Provide wooden chew toys and mineral blocks. Overgrown teeth prevent eating — a serious health issue.

Restock supplies — Check bedding, food, treats, and chews. Never run out of bedding — hamsters need deep bedding (15cm+) to burrow.

Annual vet check — Small pets often hide illness. A vet who specializes in exotic/small animals can catch issues before they become emergencies.

🍽️ What to Feed (and What to Never Feed)

Safe foods ✓

  • Broccoli, cucumber, bell pepper, zucchini
  • Cooked chicken or boiled egg (small amounts, for protein)
  • Plain cooked rice or oats
  • Sunflower seeds (sparingly — high fat)
  • Timothy hay (unlimited — great for digestion)

Never feed ✗

  • Citrus fruits — too acidic, causes digestive damage
  • Onion, garlic, leek — toxic to hamsters
  • Almonds, apple seeds — contain cyanide compounds
  • Sugary treats, candy, chocolate — causes diabetes rapidly
  • Sticky foods (peanut butter) — can impact cheek pouches

💡 Pro tip: Syrian hamsters are solitary and must live alone. Dwarf hamsters (Roborovski, Campbell's) can live in same-sex pairs if introduced as babies. Never house a Syrian with any other hamster — they will fight.

Signs Your Hamster Is Thriving

  • Active and curious in the evenings
  • Bright, clear eyes — no discharge
  • Full, rounded body — not bony or bloated
  • Clean, dry fur — no bald patches
  • Uses wheel regularly
  • Builds nests and buries food (normal hoarding behavior)
